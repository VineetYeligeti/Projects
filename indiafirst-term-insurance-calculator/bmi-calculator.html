What IS A  BMI CALCULATOR

Think a person is healthy if they are thin? Or that an overweight person is unhealthy?

When we think of health, we only consider one’s weight. However, a healthy weight is defined by the right weight to height ratio. This is known as Body Mass Index (BMI) and it is a numerical value that assesses whether a person has a healthy body weight in relation to their height. Read More

Body Mass Index does not directly measure body fat. However, it is a useful tool to identify potential health risks associated with being overweight or underweight.

A BMI calculator allows you to quickly calculate your BMI based on your weight and height.

The BMI calculator uses a BMI formula which calculates the value by dividing your weight by the square of your height. The resulting figure indicates whether you are normal weight, underweight, overweight or obese.

The BMI calculator result is fairly reliable, but it is always advisable to consult a medical professional to get a comprehensive evaluation of your health. Read Less

How to calculate your BMI

You can do a quick BMI check to see whether you need to lose, gain or maintain your weight.

1

Simply enter your exact height and weight and the BMI calculator applies the BMI formula to calculate your Body Mass Index.

2

The resulting numerical value will slot you in one of the BMI categories: underweight, normal weight, overweight, or obesity. 

What is the formula to calculate Body Mass Index

BMI calculator follows a simple formula, which can be used for both, men and women. You need to know your weight in kilograms and your height in metres. If you know what your height is in centimetres, you can simply divide that number by 100.
BMI is calculated basis the below BMI formula: Weight (kg) ÷ (Height in (m))2
The BMI calculator for women and the BMI calculator for men is the same.

International System of Units (SI)

BMI = Weight (kg) ÷ (Height in (m))2

Example:
For a 167 cm (1.67 m) individual weighing 65 kg,
BMI = 65 / (1.67 * 1.67) = 65/ 2.78 = 23.13

Imperial System

BMI = Weight (lb) ÷ (Height (inches)) 2 * 703

Example:
For a 150-pound, 5’7” (67”) individual weighing 68 kg,
BMI = (150/ 67*67) * 703 = (150/4489) * 703 = 23.49

The ideal BMI value for women and men is the same, i.e., 18.5—24.9. Anything below 18.5 is underweight and anything over 24.9-29 is overweight. BMI values are grouped into the following weight status categories:

BMI Below 18.5 18.5-24.9 25-29.9 30-35 Over 35
Weight Status Underweight Normal weight Overweight Obese Morbid obesity

    Frequently Asked Questions

    What is the BMI formula in kg?

    The BMI formula is the same whether you weigh yourself in kilograms (kg) or pounds (lb).

    Does BMI change with age?

    BMI can change with age as our body composition constantly changes with respect to fat loss or gain, muscle mass and other factors.

    Why is BMI important?

    Body Mass Index is important as it is a good indicator of health and can forewarn you of any potential health risks, if you are not in the healthy weight range.

    What are the factors affecting BMI?

    Age, lifestyle, physical activity, diet, medical history, and family genes are some factors that affect BMI.

    What is the benefit for using body mass index calculator?

    A BMI calculator is a good indicator of health and a screening tool to identify individuals who may be at increased risk of developing certain health conditions associated with weight.

    What is good body fat percentage?

    Body fat is affected by various factors. However, the average normal range is 18-24% for men and 25-31% for women.
