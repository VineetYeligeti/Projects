How to Choose the Right Term Insurance Plan Online?

Before you choose your term insurance plan online, it is important to understand your requirements in depth. answers for your queriesfrequently asked questions

1. What are term insurance plans?

A term insurance plan is a pure protection plan that provides financial coverage to the dependants, in case of the policyholder's death. It provides protection for a definite period of time, or 'term'. Term plan is the simplest and most affordable insurance that will help you to secure your family's financial future.

2. Why should you buy IndiaFirst Anytime Plan?

A term insurance plan is the most affordable, which means you can cover your family for a substantial amount at a low premium. IndiaFirst Anytime Plan also offers other benefits against misfortunes of life such as unexpected death of the breadwinner, death caused by accident, multiple payout options, and tax savings.

3. What is the right amount of coverage for me?

When calculating your term insurance requirement, your age and current financial status are important determinants. Your term insurance coverage should ideally amount to at least 15 to 20 times your current annual income. The higher the coverage, the more secure your family's future. However, the best way to buy term insurance is by first assessing your current and future expenses and liabilities, and important life events and goals. Whatever the term insurance coverage you take, you should be able to afford the premium payment in the long run as missing a payment could lead to your policy lapsing.

4. What documents do I need to buy the online term plan?

The documents you need to purchase an online term plan are the same as when you buy term insurance from an agent. These are:

  • Salary Slips of the last 3 months from Government, MNC, or Private Limited Companies
  • Income Tax Returns of the last 3 years along with computation of income
  • Bank Statements of the last 6 months with salary entries
  • For business owners, Audited Business Account Statements of the last 3 years, including Balance Sheet and Profit & Loss Account Statements from the CA. It is mandatory to have the CA's stamp and membership number
  • Latest Form 16
  • Aadhaar Card or any Government approved identity proof / Address proof / Age proof
  • Address proof - passport or utility bill
  • Recent full-length photo

5. What is Sum Assured?

Sum Assured is the guaranteed amount that the insurance company pays to the beneficiary, on the life assured's death. It is also known as coverage amount and the premiums are paid towards this.

6. What is a Premium Paying Term?

Premium Paying Term refers to the total numbers of years that the premium is paid for towards the life cover. It is important to complete the premium payment for the entire term to keep the policy valid, so that the cover can be paid out when needed the most.

7. I am an NRI. Can I still buy the IndiaFirst Anytime Plan?

Yes. NRIs can protect their families in India by investing in the IndiaFirst Anytime Plan.

8. At what age should I buy a term insurance plan?

You can buy a term insurance plan such as the IndiaFirst Anytime Plan from the ages of 18 to 60 years. The policy covers you till age 70 years or till death, whichever comes first. However, the younger you are when you buy a term plan, the more beneficial it is as you get a substantial coverage at a low premium. If you buy term insurance in your 20s and 30s, you get the benefit of a lower premium compared to if you were to buy it when you were say, in your 40s.

9. What should the tenure of my term insurance plan be?

The tenure of your term insurance plan will depend on your desired financial goals, number of dependents and future expenses and liabilities. It is advisable to buy term insurance for the maximum duration available for your age and profile, even if you feel you may not have the need for it, as life is uncertain. You can always cancel an insurance plan say, at age 65, but if you suddenly feel the need for it at age 50, you may not be eligible and if you are, the premium will be expensive.

10. Will I get a maturity benefit if I survive the tenure of the term insurance plan?

Term insurance plan does not offer maturity benefit, only death benefit wherein the sum assured gets paid to the nominees in the event of the life assured’s death.

11. Will my premium amount change during the tenure of the policy?

The premium amount does not change unless you change your coverage amount or purchase additional covers to protect you from critical illness, or death or disability caused by accident.

12. How does smoking habits affect my online term plan premium?

Your online insurance term plan will charge you a higher premium if you are a smoker than a non-smoker. This is because of the health hazards associated with smoking, which increase your risk quotient. If you are a smoker, do not think of hiding this from the insurance company when applying for an online term plan as if something happens to you during the policy term, it will be termed as fraud and your policy could be terminated. You will lose all premiums paid in this case.

13. I am an occasional smoker. Do I still need to declare myself as a tobacco user?

Yes, because if you hide this fact and it causes a health issue, you could face legal complications and could lead to your policy being terminated.

14. What are the payout options in IndiaFirst Anytime Plan?

IndiaFirst Anytime Plan offers lumpsum payout option in the event of the life assured's death to the beneficiary.

15. What is the age limit to buy IndiaFirst Anytime Plan?

You must be at least 18 years old to buy IndiaFirst Anytime Plan.

16. Do I need a Term Plan in my 50s or 60s?

A term plan can benefit anyone at any age as it provides a financial cushion for their dependents, in case they suddenly pass away. However, it is advisable to purchase it at a younger age as the premium is lower.

17. Why should I buy a ₹ 1 crore term plan?

A ₹ 1 crore term plan means that your family is covered for that amount. It is a wise and popular choice today as it secures your family's financial future, keeping future investment and liabilities and inflation in mind. However, you can decide the cover basis your requirements. IndiaFirst Anytime Plan offers ₹ 1 crore cover at an affordable premium.

18. How many times can I change the nominee in my online term plan?

You can change the nominee in your online term plan as often as you like unless specified by the insurance provider.

19. Do you get your money back at the end of the policy term on survival?

No, you do not get your money back if you outlive the policy term.

20. Can you cash out term insurance plan?

No, you cannot cash out the term insurance plan as it only offers death benefit, not maturity benefit.

21. What if I become an NRI after purchasing a term plan?

Your term plan will continue to exist as is, should you become an NRI or move to another country during the policy term.

22. What will happen if I don't die until my term plan is over?

If you survive the policy term, you will not receive any maturity benefit as term insurance plan only offers death benefit.

23. Do I get tax benefits if I buy online term plans?

Tax benefits may be available on premiums paid and benefits receivable as per prevailing Income Tax Laws. These are subject to change from time to time as per the Government Tax laws. Please consult your tax consultant before purchasing this policy.
